Afghanistan's Future and Moving Forward


Moving Forward


Moving forward, there are a number of concerns that both Afghans and internationals, with one such concern being the maintenance of women's rights, considering how these rights were very limited when the Taliban first ruled Afghanistan. With foreign powers telling the new government to respect these rights, with the Taliban themselves even saying that they will maintain such.


But despite these promises, there have been reports of the Taliban imposing restrictions on these rights, with some being reminiscent of the first time they ruled. In the long run, women face an uncertain future, as activists and the international community face these Taliban restrictions with uncertainty that their wishes and demands will be granted.


One more problem Afghanistan faces is the worsening humanitarian and economic crisis and collapse that is still occurring to this day, as with this crisis being bad enough with the previous government, the takeover of the Taliban may have worsened this event, as some international aid and support have pulled out ever since the group took over the country. With already severe hunger and drought crises, as well as a worsening Covid-19 situation and now a loss of aid from international organizations, many more Afghans have fled the country way after the Taliban takeover in search of a better life and living conditions.


Even with the groups and organizations that are still supporting the Afghan population, the crisis has deepened into a point where the aid is not enough to support the majority of people who are in need.


With these problems in the nation, the new government may not even be able to help or alleviate the effects of such due to a number of the best qualified experts and professionals in the country in branches like the health and science departments having fled the nation due to the war and ongoing crises. Alongside this, there have been even more safety issues in the nation ever since the Taliban took power, with possible extremist and terrorist groups having the chance to flourish in the country, with some of these intensifying their attacks on Afghan civilians.


In the end, the route that Afghanistan is one filled with uncertainty, with underlying problems that have already been worsened by the Taliban takeover, the next steps and actions that this new government will take are crucial in knowing how they will combat these issues and crises as both locals and internationals witness the events continue to unfold.
